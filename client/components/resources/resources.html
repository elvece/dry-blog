Learning

    The Hard Thing About Hard Things
  • "Having moved from being a CTO at my last company to CEO at this company, which is a totally different thing for a venture-backed startup, which we are now, it’s like, it’s really just a whole new set of pressures. Where coding is super black and white, people problems, which is largely what CEO’s deal with, is so shades of grey. It’s nuanced. There are feelings involved, you can’t just like, solve the problem, you can’t solve the person. So, that’s been really hard for me, and the Hard Thing about Hard Things has really helped me think through the problems of being a CEO."
    • - Char Genevier

Community

    Glassbreakers
  • "It’s a mentorship platform that one of my good friends started. You get paired up with other women who are similar, and can meet them [or] chat over email. [It’s] all over the country. I’ve been paired with women not only in Denver. Some I regularly meet with to get coffee. They also have a lot of good articles. I think it’s really cool because they are now mostly going to be working with big enterprise companies, [working] to implement this platform for them. Right now, it is open to anyone and once you are in, you get matched up with people. It’s called Glassbreakers because, you know, it’s about women breaking the glass ceiling."
    • - Kinsey Ann Durham

    Calm App
  • "It’s an app for guided meditation, it’s not like you just set a timer. You pick a focus or sleep meditation and doing that everyday has completely changed my ability to deal with stress.
